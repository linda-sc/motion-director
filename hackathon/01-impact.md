# Impact (35%)

## The thesis

Western animation pipelines (Disney, Illumination) optimized for **simplified character
designs + rich motion** — full arcs, anticipation, overshoot, follow-through, weight, ease.

Eastern pipelines (traditional anime) optimized for **gorgeous, detailed posed keyframes +
economical motion** — held frames, fewer in-betweens, a lower *effective* framerate that
puts the budget into picturesque "frame-worthy" stills rather than continuous motion.

Neither is wrong — they're different optimizations for different constraints. But the
fastest-growing, highest-margin segment of animation today is the **premium blend** of both:
the posed beauty of anime *with* the motion mechanics of Western studios.

Reference points: *Arcane*, *Spider-Verse*, modern Ufotable fight choreography (*Demon Slayer*).

## The gap

There is no **director-facing** tool to *specify* that blend before production. Motion
direction today is:

- verbal notes and references ("make it weightier, like this clip"),
- hand-drawn timing charts and arcs on paper,
- or expensive 3D previz in Maya/Blender — overkill for a 2D notes pass.

That iteration loop is **days**. For a director coordinating an external studio, every
round-trip is a meeting, a misread note, a re-draw.

## What this tool does

Collapses that loop to **minutes**. A director sketches/speaks the motion intent on a
mannequin, the tool produces a playable preview *and* a studio-ready brief in the studio's
own vocabulary. The motion language (arcs, anticipation, overshoot, the East/West blend)
is built into the instrument.

## Who benefits

- **Animation/previz directors** — the primary user; faster, clearer motion direction.
- **2D studios receiving the work** — unambiguous briefs instead of prose + vibes.
- **Studios/IP owners** — cheaper, faster pre-production iteration on a high-cost stage.

## The unfair advantage (say this out loud in the pitch)

**The builder is the actual customer.** This is the exact problem faced when directing the
anime studios our company works with. Most hackathon impact claims are hypothetical; this
one ships into a real workflow Monday.

## Scope honesty (pre-empt the skeptic)

- This is for **2D animation direction**, not rigging game sprites or auto-generating final
  frames. The output is *director's intent* — a brief a human animator draws from.
- Industry is entertainment — but it's a large, global, high-margin one, and motion
  pre-production is a genuine, expensive bottleneck within it.
